Research Projects


Our group focuses on the experimental studies related to spintronics and quantum materials, including:

1Spin current as a probe for quantum materials

We use the spin current for the probe of insulating quantum materials and the quantum interfaces, which can not be probed by other techniques easily.

1) Spin dynamics in superconductor/ferromagnetic heterostructures. Using spin current, we can probe the spin dynamics of the superconducting films from the magnetization dynamics due to the interface exchange interaction.




Ref: Yunyan Yao et al. Physical Review B 97, 224414 (2018)

2) Spin superfluidity. Using the spin current, we observed the experimental signatures for the spin superfluid ground state in canted antiferromagnetic Cr2O3 thin films.


Ref: Yuan Wei et al. Science Advances 4: eaat1098 (2018)


2Growth of quantum materials thin films

We use thin film growth techniques (MBE and PLD) to grow quantum materials and interfaces.

1) Spin polarized interfaces. The interface states are buried between two oxide materials, which can be ferromagnetic using ferromagnetic insulators, such as EuO, and Fe3O4, and Cr2O3.


Ref: Hongrui Zhang et al. Physical Review Letters 121, 116803 (2018)

2) Novel ferromagnetic oxide materials, such as topological Hall effect in oxide thin films.



Ref: Yu Yun et al. Physical Review Materials 2, 034201 (2018)

3Quantum materials for spintronics applications

We take the advantages of the special spin properties of quantum materials, and use them for the spintronics applications.

1) Quantum materials for spin and charge conversion. The generation and detection of spin current are the central topics in spintronics. These quantum materials, such as topological insulators, 2DEG, and nonconllinear antiferromagnets, provide special platform for the spin and charge conversion.


Ref: Wei Han et al. npj Quantum Materials 3, 27 (2018)

2) 2D Ferromagnetism. When the ferromagnetism is confined in the 2D limit, new interesting properties could be observed, which might be useful for future spintronics applications.



Ref: Wenyu Xing et al. Physical Review X, 9(1), 011026 (2019)
